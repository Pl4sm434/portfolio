Chapter 01

The Motor Pool

June 2023. A young Marine stands in front of a piece of engineer equipment that won't start. There's no Stack Overflow for this. There's a schematic, a multimeter, and the expectation that it gets fixed.

As an Engineer Equipment Electrical Systems Technician, Johan learned to trace faults methodically — run the diagnostics, check the circuits, isolate the failure, prove the repair. Done consistently, that discipline cut equipment downtime by 20%.

He also learned something no classroom teaches: how to train others, how to stay calm when everything is urgent, and how to own a problem until it's actually solved.

Chapter 02

The Pivot

Diagnosing machines raised a question: what if I could build the systems, not just repair them?

So he enrolled at De Anza College in Computer Science while continuing to serve in the reserves. Java 17 Masterclass. C++ on Codecademy. JavaScript. Unreal Engine fundamentals. The multimeter became a debugger — and the method transferred perfectly: observe, hypothesize, test, verify.

Transfer target: 2026.

Chapter 03

The Build

The projects started small and real. A multi-class Java game to learn OOP the honest way. A wedding seating app with a hard deadline and users in formalwear — scanned via QR code, searched in real time, shipped on the day. A Python order processor that killed a spreadsheet chore. A password checker that explains instead of scolds.

None of them pretend to be more than they are. All of them work, and each one taught something that made the next one better.

That's the pattern: diagnose, learn, build, repeat. The next chapter is an internship or entry-level role — and it isn't written yet.
